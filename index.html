Week 8: Festival of Economics

Growth for Good

Alessio Terzi argues that people - particularly the young - feel increasingly frustrated by unsustainable and unequal economic growth. Chart 8 and 9 explore whether this is reflected in attitudes towards policy-makers.

Chart 8 demonstrates that there is no clear trend in levels of trust in governance over time for these countries.

Chart 9 shows that in the last 20 years, an increasing share of Americans are not confident in the US government - which corroborates Terzi's argument. However, it also suggests that low confidence is more prevalent amongst older groups. Therefore, this survey's results contradict the idea that young people in particular are frustrated with policymakers.

Week 10-11: Machine Learning

Supervised Learning

I have used linear regression to examine a potential relationship between the distribution of online ads posted by the Democratic Party and US population by state. The resulting line suggests a positive relationship, whereby the Democratic Party tends to post more ads in states with a higher population. My Colab Code.

Unsupervised Learning

I have used text clustering to analyse Facebook and Instagram adverts from 2021 which mention 'Brexit'. Clustering with text data required cleaning the text and vectorizing it using the sklearn library 'tfidvectorizer'. I aimed to explore whether distinguishable clusters appeared, which could be considered "pro" or "anti" Brexit. I created a wordcloud as part of my initial exploration of the data.

Overall, checking the key words of the clusters revealed no clearly distinguishable pro or anti-Brexit sentiment. Nonetheless, experimenting with the number of clusters does reveal the range of Brexit-related issues featured in the ads, for example, some clusters featured themes of business, farming, and pollution.
